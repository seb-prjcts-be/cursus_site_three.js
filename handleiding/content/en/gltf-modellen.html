So far you built everything from basic shapes. With the GLTFLoader you load ready-made 3D models - from Blender, from Sketchfab, or from the examples folder of your clone. Including their animations.

What you will learn
  • glTF (.glb) is the standard format for 3D on the web - "the jpeg of 3D"
  • GLTFLoader is an addon, loading is asynchronous with a callback
  • The loaded model is an ordinary scene branch: scaling, rotating and grouping work as always
  • You play built-in animations with an AnimationMixer

The loading recipe

import { GLTFLoader } from 'three/addons/loaders/GLTFLoader.js';

new GLTFLoader().load('model.glb', (gltf) => {
  const model = gltf.scene;       // the tree of meshes
  model.scale.setScalar(0.05);    // models are rarely at "your" scale
  scene.add(model);
});

The same pattern as with fonts and textures: load, and add in the callback. The gltf object contains more than just the meshes: gltf.animations is a list of built-in animation clips.

Watch out

Seeing nothing after loading? The model is almost always too big, too small, or not where you are looking. Start with model.scale.setScalar and try powers of 10 (0.01, 0.1, 1, 10). A GridHelper next to it helps you judge the scale.

Try it: a flying flamingo

The flamingo comes from your local clone and has a built-in flying animation. The AnimationMixer is the "play button": you feed it the elapsed time every frame:

The mixer wants getDelta() (time since the previous frame), not getElapsed(). That way the animation plays at the same speed on every screen - the same framerate-independent idea as in Animation & time.

Where do you get models?

  • Your clone - three.js/examples/models/gltf/ contains dozens of test models: a horse, a duck, a Ferrari.
  • Make your own - Blender exports straight to .glb (File → Export → glTF). The royal road for your own work.
  • Online - Sketchfab and Poly Haven have thousands of free models. Check the licence and credit the maker.
Did you know

Some .glb files are extra compressed with "Draco". For those you attach a DRACOLoader to your GLTFLoader - look up the example webgl_loader_gltf in your clone if you run into such a file and the loader throws an error.

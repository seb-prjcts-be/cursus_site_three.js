Atmosphere is what turns a collection of shapes into a world: a background colour that plays along, fog that adds depth, and a floor that anchors the space. Three small interventions, big effect.

What you will learn
  • scene.background can be a colour, but also a texture
  • scene.fog makes objects in the distance fade towards a colour
  • The trick: give the fog the same colour as the background, and the horizon melts away
  • Fog(color, near, far) is linear, FogExp2(color, density) feels more natural

Fog in one line

scene.background = new THREE.Color(0x141414);
scene.fog = new THREE.Fog(0x141414, 5, 25);
//                        colour  from   fully gone

From a distance of 5 units everything starts to fade; at 25 units it has fully dissolved into the background colour. Because both colours are identical, you see no hard horizon - only distance.

In p5 you built this effect yourself with alpha based on distance; here it is one property of the scene, and it works automatically on every material.

Try it: an endless field

The classic "infinite world" feeling: a field of pillars vanishing into the fog. Try dragging the fog values up or down:

The camera keeps sliding 4 units forward and then jumps back - exactly the distance between two rows of pillars. Thanks to the fog you never see the jump: an infinite loop with twenty objects. This is a classic game trick.

More atmosphere knobs

  • FogExp2 - new THREE.FogExp2(color, 0.08): exponential fog, thicker the further you look. One density knob instead of near/far.
  • Background photo - scene.background = new THREE.TextureLoader().load('sky.jpg'). For real 360° skies, look up "equirectangular skybox" later.
  • Coloured light - the fastest mood-maker there is: a blue DirectionalLight is night, an orange one is sunset. Combine with an AmbientLight in a contrasting colour.
Did you know

Fog works inside the shader of every standard material and therefore costs almost nothing. It may well be the cheapest "production value" in all of three.js: two lines of code and your scene suddenly has depth and mystery.
